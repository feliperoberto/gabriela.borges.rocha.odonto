# Visual Identity Guide
## Dental Practice — São José dos Campos/SP
 
---
 
## 1. Brand Essence
 
### Brand Profile
A young female dentist, 30 years old, with 12+ years of hands-on experience as a dental auxiliary and a recent graduate. This unique combination communicates **deep practical knowledge paired with fresh academic preparation** — a powerful differentiator in the market.
 
### Brand Personality
| Trait | Expression |
|---|---|
| **Warm & Welcoming** | Approachable tone, soft palette, rounded visual elements |
| **Confident & Experienced** | Clean layouts, mature typography, no "cutesy" clichés |
| **Modern & Fresh** | Contemporary design language, digital-first thinking |
| **Feminine without being fragile** | Strength through elegance, not through clinical coldness |
 
### Brand Voice
- First person, warm, educational
- Avoids overly clinical jargon with patients
- Empathetic — she *knows* what it's like on both sides of the chair
- Confident but never arrogant
### Core Brand Story (use across channels)
> "Com mais de 12 anos vivendo a odontologia por dentro — e agora como dentista — eu entendo cada detalhe do seu cuidado."
 
This narrative of **"from the inside out"** is a rare and authentic differentiator. Use it.

| **Pure White** | `#FFFAF7` | 255, 250, 247 | Page backgrounds (warm white, not cold) |
| **Dark Espresso** | `#2E1A10` | 46, 26, 16 | Small text / fine print for better contrast |

### Color Usage Rules
- **Never** use Blush (`#F2DBCE`) for body text — insufficient contrast
- **Never** place Rose (`#D98989`) text on Blush (`#F2DBCE`) — fails WCAG AA
- Chocolate (`#592A19`) on Blush (`#F2DBCE`) = ✅ primary readable combination
- Chocolate (`#592A19`) on White (`#FFFAF7`) = ✅ highest contrast pair
- **Maximum 3 colors per composition**; use one dominant, one supporting, one accent
- Digital backgrounds: prefer `#FFFAF7` (warm white) over pure `#FFFFFF` (cold)

## 3. Typography
 
### Recommended Google Font Pairings
 
#### Option A — Elegant & Warm (Recommended)
- **Display / Logo / Headings:** [Cormorant Garamond](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Cormorant+Garamond) — Semi-Bold (600) & Italic
- **Body / UI / Captions:** [DM Sans](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/DM+Sans) — Regular (400) & Medium (500)
*Why:* Cormorant Garamond is a refined, high-contrast serif that brings femininity and sophistication without being delicate. DM Sans is geometric and highly legible, providing modern clarity for body content.
 
#### Option B — Modern & Approachable
- **Display / Headings:** [Playfair Display](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Playfair+Display) — Bold (700)
- **Body / UI:** [Nunito](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Nunito) — Regular (400) & Semi-Bold (600)
*Why:* Playfair brings editorial elegance; Nunito's rounded terminals feel friendly and approachable — great for patient communication.
 
#### Option C — Clean & Professional
- **Display / Headings:** [Lora](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Lora) — Medium (500) & Bold (700)
- **Body / UI:** [Source Sans 3](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Source+Sans+3) — Regular (400) & Semi-Bold (600)
*Why:* Lora is warm and organic; Source Sans 3 is Adobe's workhorse for digital readability. This pair is the most "clinical-professional" of the three.
 
### Type Scale
 
| Element | Font | Weight | Size | Color |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **H1 — Hero** | Cormorant Garamond | 600 | 48–64px | `#592A19` |
| **H2 — Section** | Cormorant Garamond | 500 | 32–40px | `#592A19` |
| **H3 — Subsection** | DM Sans | 600 | 20–24px | `#A6634B` |
| **Body** | DM Sans | 400 | 16px | `#592A19` |
| **Caption / Small** | DM Sans | 400 | 13–14px | `#A6634B` |
| **Button** | DM Sans | 600 | 14–16px | `#FFFAF7` on `#592A19` |
 
### Typography Rules
- Line-height: 1.6–1.7 for body; 1.2 for headings
- Letter-spacing: +0.5px on uppercase labels, 0 on body
- Never use more than 2 font families in a single piece
- Minimum body size: 16px digital, 10pt print

## 4. Logo Guidelines
 
### Logo Concept Direction
The logo should communicate: **warmth, expertise, femininity, modernity**.
 
#### Recommended Approach
- **Wordmark-first**: A custom typographic treatment of the dentist's name using Cormorant Garamond (or a similar high-contrast serif) as the base, with subtle customization on key letters
- **Optional icon/monogram**: A minimal symbol that can stand alone for social avatars, favicons, and watermarks — abstract tooth shape, smile curve, or stylized initials
- **Avoid**: cartoon teeth, literal tooth icons, generic dental crosses, clip-art smiles
#### Logo Variations Required
1. **Primary Logo** — Full name + icon (horizontal)
2. **Stacked Logo** — Name above/below icon (vertical, for square formats)
3. **Icon Only** — Monogram or symbol (for avatars, favicons, watermarks)
4. **One-Color** — Chocolate (`#592A19`) on light backgrounds
5. **Reversed** — White/Blush on dark backgrounds
#### Logo Clear Space
- Minimum clear space around logo = height of the capital letter "D" in the logotype on all sides
- Never place the logo on busy photo backgrounds without a solid or semi-transparent overlay
#### Logo Misuse (Don'ts)
- Don't rotate or skew
- Don't change the color palette
- Don't add drop shadows or outlines
- Don't place on low-contrast backgrounds
- Don't stretch or distort proportions
- Don't use the icon below 24px without testing legibility

## 5. Illustration & Graphic Style
 
### Photography Direction
- **Tone**: Warm, natural light. Slightly warm white balance to match the palette
- **Subject**: Candid, confident moments — not stock-photo smiles. Show her working, consulting, in her space
- **Color grading**: Lift shadows slightly, warm mid-tones. Desaturate blues/greens. Enhance warm skin tones
- **Backgrounds**: Clean, light, minimal. Blurred clinic interiors in warm tones
### Illustration Style
- **Line art**: Thin, continuous, single-weight lines in Chocolate (`#592A19`) or Terracotta (`#A6634B`)
- **Style**: Minimalist, organic, slightly hand-drawn feel — not geometric/corporate
- **Fill**: Selective use of Blush, Nude, or Rose as fills — never fully colored illustrations
- **Subjects**: Abstract dental elements (smile curves, tooth silhouettes), botanical/organic accents (leaves, soft arcs)
- **Avoid**: Realistic anatomical illustrations, cartoonish styles, overly complex graphics
### Graphic Patterns & Textures
- Subtle grain/noise overlays on solid color blocks (5–8% opacity)
- Soft arc or wave motifs echoing a smile curve
- Organic blob shapes as background accents in Blush or Nude at low opacity
- Fine line separators instead of heavy rules
### Iconography
- Style: Outlined, rounded corners, 1.5–2px stroke weight
- Color: Terracotta (`#A6634B`) or Chocolate (`#592A19`)
- Size: Minimum 24×24px; target 32×32px for UI

## 6. Digital Applications
 
### Social Media
- **Profile picture**: Icon-only logo version on Blush background
- **Post templates**: 2–3 recurring layouts. Consistent placement of logo, consistent typography
- **Instagram grid**: Alternate between photography (warm-toned), educational carousels (Blush bg + Chocolate text), and quote/tip posts (Rose accent)
- **Stories**: Use Blush/White backgrounds, Chocolate text, Terracotta highlights
### Website
- Background: `#FFFAF7`
- Cards/sections: `#F2DBCE` at 40% opacity or solid
- Primary buttons: `#592A19` fill, `#FFFAF7` text, 8px border-radius
- Secondary buttons: `#592A19` outline, transparent fill
- Links: `#A6634B`, underline on hover
### Business Card
- Front: Logo centered on `#FFFAF7`, minimal
- Back: Full Blush (`#F2DBCE`) background, contact info in Chocolate, accent line in Terracotta
- Finish: Matte with spot UV on the logo (premium feel)
### Clinic Signage
- Primary sign: Logo on white or Blush background
- Interior: Framed prints using brand illustration style
- Wayfinding: DM Sans Medium, Terracotta on white
